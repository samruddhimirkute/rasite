TIPS AND STRATEGIES FOR MANAGING RA

If RA is diagnosed timely, symptoms and disease progression can be controlled with pharmacological treatment and optimal functioning of joints can be maintained.
Reducing exposure to inhaled silica, and lifestyle-related behaviors (smoking, healthy nutrition, physical activity, body weight, good dental hygiene)

Pharmacological Treatment:
It is recommended to treat RA with at least one specific anti-rheumatic medication.
Disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs) are the primary treatment for RA.
DMARDs: methotrexate, sulfasalazine, leflunomide, hydroxychloroquine, TNF inhibitors (certolizumab, adalimumab, infliximab, and etanercept), abatacept, anakinra, auranofin, rituximab, tocilizumab. These drugs helps in the treatment of RA.
Glucocorticoids, steroids, paracetamol, non-NSAID, COX-2 inhibitors are considered as anti-inflammatory and analgesic agents that helps to reduce pain and inflammations.

Physical Activity:
Physical activity (regular exercise) is beneficial for people with rheumatoid arthritis since it increases the production of synovial fluid, which lubricates the joints and reduces friction.
Occupational therapy plays a positive role in improving functional ability in people sufering from rheumatoid arthritis.
Physiotherapy includes cold and heat application, electronic stimulation, and hydrotherapy.
Wearing a splint for a short time around a painful joint reduces pain and swelling.
Healthy Eating:
A healthy and nutritious diet that includes a balance of calories, protein, and calcium is important for maintaining overall health.
Topical products like creams, gels, or stick-on patches can temporary help to ease the pain in a joint or muscle.
Supplements: omega-3 fish oil supplements may help with rheumatoid arthritis.
