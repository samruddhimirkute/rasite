CAUSES

The causes of rheumatoid arthritis are unknown.

Development of RA :
• Immune cells cause inflammation of synovium. (In the inner lining of the joint)
• This inflammation becomes chronic (long long-lasting), and the synovium thickens due to an increase of cells & production of proteins which can lead to pain, redness, and warmth.
• As RA progresses, the thickened and inflamed synovium is pushed further, into the joint and affects the cartilage and bone within the joint.
• As the joint capsule stretches, the forces cause changes within the joint structure.
• The surrounding muscles, ligaments, and tendons that support and stabilize the joint become weak over time and do not work normally. This leads to more pain and joint damage.

While the cause of rheumatoid arthritis is not clear, it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors :
• Genes : Genes that controls the activity of immune system may lead to RA. However, genes are not the only factor in the development of RA.
• Environment : Cigarette smoke may trigger rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, some factors such as inhalants, bacteria, viruses, gum disease, and lung disease may play a role in the development of RA.
• Sex hormones :
Women are more likely than men to develop rheumatoid arthritis.
The disease may increase during pregnancy and flare after pregnancy.
